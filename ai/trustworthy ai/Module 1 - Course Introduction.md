## Overview
The course **"Responsible AI and Ethics"** focuses on understanding and managing the ethical, social, and technical challenges of **artificial intelligence** (AI) and **machine learning** (ML) in organizational contexts.

**Course Structure** and **Modules**
- The course is divided intro three modules: **Bias (Human and Machine)**, **Responsible AI**, and **Case Studies**.
- Each module addresses key aspects such as sources of bias, ethical, frameworks, transparency, fairness, accountability, and real-world AI applications.

**Bias in AI Systems**
- Explores the origins and impacts of both human and algorithmic biases in AI models.
- Provides strategies for identifying, measuring, and mitigating bias to promote fairness and equity in AI decision-making.

**Responsible AI Principles**
- Covers ethical principles essential for responsible AI development, including transparency, fairness, and accountability.
- Emphasizes creating AI systems that are ethical, socially responsible, and sustainable.

**Case Studies and Practical Insights**
- Presents real-world examples of generative AI applications across various industries.
- Analyzes successes, challenges, and practical implications to inform effective AI project management and deployment.

**Learning Outcomes**
- Learners will gain a comprehensive understanding of bias, fairness, accountability, and societal impacts of AI.
- Equipped to develop and lead AI initiatives that balance innovation with ethical responsibility and sustainability.
